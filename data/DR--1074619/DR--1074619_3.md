Another reason for the new riser placement is that we are using the shotgun approach for performances. The shotgun formation provides all of you a much better chance to hear the wonderful harmony that the Minneapolis Commodores create. Why do you sing Barbershop? Of course, to hear the HARMONY! I have even used some basic math to space various parts as efficiently as possible. That gets difficult with significantly different numbers of singers among the four sections.
The final argument for the new riser placement is that it raises the bar for every Commodore on the risers. When you are surrounded by voice parts other than your own, it challenges everyone to really, really know their music quite well! It doesn’t take a rocket scientist to understand that the first piece of a successful choral ensemble is, ”Know your words and notes.” Wow! What a concept.
