# Energy - Giver? Taker? Sharer?

## By Denny Maas, President

![Figure](fileoutpart9.png)

Each time we sing together, I see our directors sharing their energy with us. Regardless of what happened to them that day or how tired they may really be, each freely shares all of Denny Maas his energy as if it were the first thing he was doing that day. They move quickly to their position, make a quick spin to face us with a wonderful charming smile. When they speak, their voices are filled with enthusiasm. This sharing continues until our final song and we leave the building.
Their actions remind me of the time I spent training auditors on how to audit IBM’s operating system software. The challenge was to keep the class awake for two days while teaching about a topic that is tedious and boring. At the end of each day, I had left all the energy I started with in the class room. I was drained but the auditors were awake. Mission accomplished.
This has made me question whether we are draining energy from our directors or are we sharing our energy? As a result, I asked our Aussie visitors regarding their observations of the chorus. Both John and Paul are music historians and they spend much of their time watching musicians perform. They both agreed that many of us are often pretty laid back and not really giving back much energy to the directors. This supported my own observation.

## By Paul Wigley, Director

![Figure](fileoutpart10.png)

I want to thank all of you for your patience, acceptance, celebration, and excitement about the new riser placement concept. (Well, at least the majority of you have celebrated it—ha ha!) There are a number of rea-Paul Wigley sons why I am excited about this new singing arrangement. First of all, it is simply a good idea to mix things up every now and then. While it is very easy to conduct rehearsal after rehearsal with no changes, eventually things get a bit stale. It’s a very well-known fact among choral directors that ANY change in standing order will bring about immediate improvement, at least for a while.
