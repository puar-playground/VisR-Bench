### A Visitors Night to remember

#### By Blake Wanger, Program Vice President

![Figure](fileoutpart27.png)

On Tuesday evening, June 6, the Commodores put their collective best feet forward and welcomed several enthusiastic guests (six of whom officially registered and took home the guest packets), to the premier of the Min-Blake Wanger neapolis Chapter’s newly-formatted Guests Night, introducing them to a well-orchestrated view of the goings-on in a typical chapter meeting.
The well-planned, revitalized program put together by Membership VP, Harvey Weiss, with input from the Board members [especially Program VP, Blake Wanger], and the Music Committee, ran smoothly without a hitch.
Emcee Blake Wanger called the house to order and evening began with the chorus greeting the assembled visitors with their newly-adopted welcoming/closing song, Hello/ Goodbye (by the Beatles), led by assistant director, Gary Jacobson. Each guest was then introduced individually either by the Commodore sponsoring him or, for those who sought us out on their own, by a member appointed to do so. Not surprisingly as it

## There’s a Meeting Here Tonight
At May 16’s chapter meeting the Commodores were visited by two Aussies, John Kearney and Paul Markota, whom Denny and Sharyn Maas had met on their recent trip to the Island Continent.
Both are lecturers on the history of modern music. Their initial meeting with Denny and Sharyn actually occurring on a Royal Caribbean Lines cruise from Australia to New Zealand on which both men were lecturing at the time. Paul regularly contracts with the line to lecture at sea.

# A LETTER
To all Commodores
My most sincere gratitude goes out to all who were in attendance at last evening’s Guest Night. You made it a fun, entertaining and informative night. I think our guests got the idea that we are a fun group to sing with.
Special kudos must go to all who had some responsibilities in performing or informing. You know who you are, and Ringmaster Blake [Wanger] made it all go so smoothly and proficiently. He is really good at what he does for us.
I am looking forward to seeing some of these fellows return in the next few weeks. They will each receive a short note thanking them for attending and encouraging them to pay us an early return visit to experience a regular chapter meeting and rehearsal. Also they might get a feel for the real Barbershop world, if they would come to Osseo next Tuesday.
I was really glad to see Eric Nelsen back in the groove and look forward to his numerous talents in the coming weeks and months.
Harvey
