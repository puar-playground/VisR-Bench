# VOCALITY- From The Porch for Youth In Harmony

![Figure](fileoutpart30.png)

## By Jay Althof, Vocality Tenor
A few years ago we (the Vocality Quartet) were commenting at a rehearsal about the number of friends and family who told us they wished they could hear us perform in the Twin Cities area. While we have been fortunate to perform many, many places across the mid-west and beyond, it had been some time since we had been asked to perform locally. Then last summer we thought it would be fun to hold a free concert “From The Porch” of Lake Avenue Studios in White Bear Lake. (Lake Avenue Studios being a euphemism for Jay’s home.) So we set a date and let our friends and family know hoping for good weather.
By chance, a couple of weeks before the show, we became aware of a fund raiser that the Great Northern Union (GNU) Barbershop chapter was holding to assist one of their members, Jermaine Fritz, with legal support for his pending immigration to the United States We thought it would be a nice opportunity to let our audience know they could help support Jermaine, if they chose, through a freewill offering.
The weather was beautiful last year and we had a fine time performing.
and we had a great time performing. The audience seemed to really enjoy it and they generously contributed over $500. Over the following months we had many people ask if we were going to do another concert

From The Porch this coming summer. We said, “Absolutely!”
Well, for the last several years a group of Twin City Barbershoppers, calling themselves, The Friday Lunch Bunch That Meets on Thursday (TFLBTMOT), with representatives from all of the area chapters, has been sponsoring an annual Barbershop Open at a local golf club. The express purpose of the Open has been to raise money for the Y IH program, an endeavor that provided room and board expenses for 500 young men competing in the Y outh Chorus Festival at the Barbershop Harmony Society’s Midwinter Convention in Reno, last January.
At last fall’s LOL District Conven-
tion, Vocality learned of a special fund being created as a memorial for Barbershop icon, Dr. Jim Richards, its main purpose being to support the Youth In Harmony program of the Barbershop Harmony Society and in which TFLBTMOT was quite active.

Almost instantly we thought: Our next concert From The Porch would be a great opportunity to help raise funds for this very worthy cause. The guys from TFLBTMOT had the foundation work started with the support of the Land O’ Lakes District and Harmony Foundation.
So now, Vocality has the opportunity to help fund the foundation and hope many, many Barbershoppers and their friends and families will join us on July 22 between 3:00 and 5:00 p.m. at 4671 Lake Avenue in White Bear Lake to hear and watch Vocality perform and also make donations. Bring your lawn chair and any beverage and join us for a great time!
Editor’s Note: Special thanks to Jay, Tony, David, and Tom from the members of TFLBTMOT for your touch of class. As with your fine music, your gesture here is widely appreciated and will inspire others to support our talented youth.
