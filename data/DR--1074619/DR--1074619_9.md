# Georgia Grind

## By Jim Erickson, Bucking Baritone

![Figure](fileoutpart46.png)

I am writing this article to confirm that I have been in line with the trends of the universe in my pursuing the mythical secrets of the origins of the Georgia Grind. Whatever it is, the Jim Erickson Grind is veiled in the haziness of myth, only a tiny part of which have I been able to pierce. But bucking a universal intelligence, pierce I did. Now let me tell you what spurred this thinking after reading a fairly recent article by Tim Duff in the Lakeshore Weekly News (March 16, 2017) Tim tells that technology is overtaking truth in cyberspace.
He called upon the wisdom of John Maynard Keynes, the great and prescient demand-side economist. John, anticipating the environmental debate, said, “We are capable of shutting off the sun and stars because they don’t pay a dividend. The enemy of the truth is very often not the lie: deliberate, contrived and dishonest; but the myth, persistent, persuasive and unrealistic. Belief in myths allows the comfort of opinion without the comfort of thought.”
Tim goes on to ask, “Are we beholden to each other, must we take care of each other, or is every man for himself? The Internet, like the Colt 45 of the Old West, has become the great equalizer, when technology overtakes truth. Man cheats in his own way and he is only honest who is not discovered.”
Now, I have been writing this Georgia Grind stuff for well over ten years. In fact the favorite song, Georgia Grind On My Mind, was written and sung by Ray Charles in

Now I know some of you have been hard-pressed to winnow out if there is any truth in the chaff of the Grind. Even one little truth. Well, good luck with that. Like the Herman the German statue on the bluffs of the burg of New Ulm, Minnesota, I stand for thright and bold in proclaiming that my stories have not been myth. Ooooorrrr have they? Would I rob you, my fellow reader, of the comfort of opinion without the discomfort of thought. I think not. My aim has always been to think of the comfort of the reader. Kind of the Marriot of the article world. A chocolate-on-your-pillow comfort as you drift to the land of myth.
On the other hand, there seems to be an element of cosmic truth in every myth. Just as I have pursued the origins of the Georgia Grind, I urge you to sift through the GG myths and arrive at the remote, snow-blown Siberian railroad station that represents the barren, teeth-shattering truth. Or just have a Captain Morgan Spiced Rum and Cola in Grand Cayman and seek truth in the bottom of a tall glass. It seems to work either way.
The Barbershop Bucket List is no myth; and holding fast to that which is good is one of those guides that lives easily in both the world of truth and the comfort of myths. Stay awake for many reasons, but one is to miss as little of the good life as you can. And by the time I see you again, I may be beginning the transition from practically all-human man, to transformer man with high tech, replaceable parts. But that is just for another day.
