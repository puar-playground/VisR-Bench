Next on the docket Dave Bechard directed the standing Commodores as they serenaded their wives and friends with a chorus of Let Me Call You Sweetheart.
As the senior-most past Barbershop-per of the Year present, Bob Spong was commissioned to draw the names of the lucky ladies who would take home the Aveda gift products the Ortenburgers had arranged for, followed by a $50-dollar VISA gift card won by Jamye Casperson.
At last it became time to unveil the 2017 Minneapolis Chapter’s Barber-shopper of the Year. Following the introduction of the past BOTYs in attendance and in a departure from past years in an effort to make the evening more of a ladies and guests affair, the “skit” plan had been shelved. Thus, the presentation was conducted by Mark Ortenburger beginning with his announcement of Dan True as the honoree followed by his simple reading of the prose lasered on the award plaque.
With the help of Conrad Ward and his mother, Kim, the son and wife of the deceased 2017 BOTY recipient, Bill Ward, Dan was presented with the plaque while his wife, Karen, accepted a floral bouquet. The applause was thunderous and the congratulations plethoric, with well-deserved “Well dones!” in heartfelt tribute to Dan and Karen.
To summarize, the evening was certainly remarkable thanks to the extra -hard work, (from securing the venue, sending out the invitations, keeping track of the responses, selecting the menu, and planning the program, to crafting the table decorations), Mark and Cathy put forth to make it so successful. Thanks so much, you two, for a memorable celebration so well done ala the Ortenburger style.
Editor’s Note: In what was an unscheduled total surprise to virtually everyone present, Cathy Ortenburger was presented with the special gift of an engraved jewelry box for her loving support provided to Mark throughout his noteworthy Barbershop odyssey. The engraving read as follows:

## Catherine Ortenburger … -

## CommoDear … 2017
And what better way to honor a supremely dedicated Barbershopper than to recognize the loving devotion (by thought and deed) of his wife. Perhaps this could become an annual award at future Appreciation events as there are any number of Commodore partners also have earned such recognition.
