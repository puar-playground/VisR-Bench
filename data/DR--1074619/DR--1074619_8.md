# Another Small-world Barbershopping Experience

## By Bob Dykstra, Curmudgeon-at-Large

![Figure](fileoutpart42.png)

The Society for the Preservation and Encouragement of Barber Shop Quartet Singing in America was founded on April 11, 1938. Consequently, we Bar-bershoppers now celebrate each Bob Dykstra April 11 as Barbershop Quartet Day.
I personally celebrated this year’s event by harmonizing a few songs and talking a great deal about our prior quartet experiences with three fellow Barbershop octogenarians, Jim Angell, Joel Kleschold, and Ron Thomley. One of the ways the Barbershop Harmony Society publicizes the event is to make arrangements with the television morning news show, Fox and Friends, to feature a top-notch Barbershop quartet during their April 11 telecast. I’m not sure how long this has been going on, but during the past two years (2015 and 2016), former BHS quartet gold medalists, Crossroads and Storm Front, represented our Society and current Sweet Adeline fifth-place medalist, ClassRing, represented the distaff side of Barbershop quartetting. This year’s featured quartet on Fox and Friends was a mixed quartet of teenagers from Waukesha, Wisconsin who call themselves Vintage Mix. The quartet includes three sixteen-year-old girls and a sixteen-year-old boy, all of whom share the same last name. Anika, Ian, Julia, and Kelsey Shoppach just happen to be quadruplets. They sing a variety of music but developed a special affinity for Barbershop harmony after attend- ing an annual show of the Midwest Vocal Express from nearby Greendale, one of the Barbershop Society’s premier choruses.
This new-found interest coupled with outstanding talent led to Vintage Mix’s performing at the past few International Barbershop Summer and Midwinter conventions as well as at several Society district conventions and shows (including last year’s St. Croix Valley show). They have become very well known

What does this have to do with me and my choice of the phrase “small world” in the title of this piece? Well, before I joined my first Barbershop chapter 65 years ago, I sang in a quartet of recent high school graduates in Cedar Grove, Wiscon-

sin, fifty miles or so from the current home of the Vintage Mix quadruplets, and ten miles or so from a very active-at-the-time Barbershop chapter in Sheboygan. Those of us who met in Cedar Grove sang from music loaned to us by the Sheboygan chapter, so we gained an introduction to and love for Barbershop chords.
(It was this brief taste of Barbershop quartetting which led to my joining the Hut Four Quartet at Fort Riley and the Junction City, Kansas Chapter of the Society for the Preservation and Encouragement of Barber Shop Quartet Singing in America in 1953.)
One of the members of that pre-Society quartet in Cedar Grove was Conrad (Connie) Opgenorth, a good friend, and a fellow attendee of the Sheboygan County Normal School in Sheboygan Falls, Wisconsin. Wisconsin normal schools at the time were two-year teacher-training schools designed to prepare teachers for rural schools, many of which were “one-room.” Another attendee at the Normal School during my last year there, following Connie’s graduation, was his sister, Shirley, who often caught a ride with me to and from the school.
Enrollment that academic year (1951 -52) totaled 42 prospective teachers in the first-year and second-year classes combined. In leafing through the 1952 Normal School Yearbook, The Cadet, recently, I was reminded that Shirley was known for her artistic and musical talent and that she had written me a personal message in the yearbook as was a custom of the times. So imagine my surprise and delight when I discovered recently that Shirley, who passed away four years ago, was the paternal grandmother of the Shoppach quadru-plets…i.e. Vintage Mix.
Small world, isn’t it!
